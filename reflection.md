## Reflection

When comparing the "High-Energy Pop" and "Chill Lofi" profiles, the recommendations are very different. The pop profile returns energetic and upbeat songs, while the lofi profile shifts toward slower, calmer tracks. This makes sense because the energy preference is much lower for the lofi user.

Comparing "High-Energy Pop" and "Intense Rock," both profiles favor high-energy songs, but the genres differ. The pop profile returns pop songs, while the rock profile prioritizes rock songs. This shows that genre has a strong influence on the recommendations.

Comparing "Chill Lofi" and "Intense Rock," the results are almost opposite. The lofi profile prefers low-energy, relaxing songs, while the rock profile selects loud and intense tracks. This demonstrates that energy and mood strongly affect the system's output.

The most interesting comparison is between "High-Energy Pop" and the conflicting profile (pop + sad + high energy). Even though the mood preference changes to "sad," the system still recommends similar high-energy pop songs. This happens because there are few songs that match all conditions, so the system falls back on genre and energy. This shows a limitation in how the system handles conflicting preferences.
